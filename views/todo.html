Things To Do In the San Juan Islands

⛵ Watch Orcas at Lime Kiln Point State Park

Often called the “whale watch park,” Lime Kiln Point on San Juan Island offers the rare opportunity to see orcas from shore. Bring your binoculars and enjoy the scenic trails around the lighthouse.

Visit Lime Kiln Point

⛵ Wander the San Juan Islands Sculpture Park

Just outside Roche Harbor, this 20-acre park blends art and nature with over 150 unique sculptures along forested and open-air trails. Great for a peaceful walk and creative inspiration.

Explore the Sculpture Park

⛵ Visit Pelindaba Lavender Farm

Surround yourself with the soothing scent of lavender at this picturesque working farm. Stroll the rows, snap some photos, and check out their on-site shop full of handmade goods.

TripAdvisor Reviews

Visit the Pelindaba Lavender Instagram Profile

⛵ Discover American & English Camps

Explore both sides of history at these scenic National Historical Park sites tied to the “Pig War” dispute. Peaceful trails, wildlife, and panoramic views make this a memorable stop for history buffs and hikers alike.

⛵ Explore The Whale Museum

Located in Friday Harbor, The Whale Museum offers an interactive and educational look into local marine life, especially the region’s beloved orcas. Great for families and ocean lovers.

Learn More at The Whale Museum

⛵ Take in the View from Mount Constitution

Head to Orcas Island and climb (or drive) to the summit of Mount Constitution. It’s the highest point in the San Juans and offers unmatched views of Mount Baker, surrounding islands, and even Vancouver on a clear day.

⛵ Meet the Alpacas at Krystal Acres

This charming alpaca farm is a fun, feel-good stop for all ages. Say hi to the alpacas and browse cozy handmade goods in their farm store.

Visit Krystal Acres Alpaca Farm

⛵ Hike to Shark Reef Sanctuary

Located on Lopez Island, this quiet forest trail leads to dramatic sea cliffs overlooking rocky outcroppings where sea lions love to sunbathe. A great spot for photography and solitude.

More on Shark Reef Sanctuary

⛵ Catch Sunset at Judd Cove Preserve

Hidden away on Orcas Island, this lesser-known gem offers peaceful waters, tide pools, and a perfect setting for a laid-back sunset picnic.

Final Thoughts

Whether you're spotting whales, wandering lavender fields, or soaking in a sunset by the sea, the San Juan Islands are full of unforgettable moments waiting to be discovered.
